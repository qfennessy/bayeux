The Chen-Vasquez Chronicle bayeux poisson

12 panels

  1. 1865 The Chen Family Arrival

    1865 · immigration · people: Fu-hai Chen, Mei-ling Chen, Wei Chen (infant), Jian Chen, Lin-hua Chen, Hong-wei Chen, longshoremen

    In the spring of 1865, the Chen family stepped off a Pacific Mail steamer at the San Francisco wharf after a six-week voyage from Hong Kong. Father Fu-hai carried a bamboo trunk balanced on a shoulder pole, and mother Mei-ling held baby Wei against the cold Pacific wind. The harbor was crowded with gulls, longshoremen, and piled crates of tea and silk.

    Image prompt

    At a bustling 1865 San Francisco wharf, Fu-hai Chen, a weary father in rugged work clothes, balances a bamboo trunk on a shoulder pole. Beside him, Mei-ling Chen, a young mother in a simple travel-worn tunic, cradles infant Wei Chen wrapped in a thick wool blanket against the biting wind. Rough longshoremen in oilskin coats unload tea crates nearby. The misty harbor is filled with squawking gulls, creating a tense, hopeful atmosphere.

  2. 1867 Jian Chen in the Sierra Nevada

    1867 · labor · people: Jian Chen, fellow Chinese railroad laborers

    Eldest son Jian was hired onto the Central Pacific Railroad, chipping granite inside a half-finished Sierra Nevada tunnel. He swung a heavy hammer by lantern light beside other Chinese laborers, their queues tied up under knit caps. Beyond the tunnel mouth, snowdrifts leaned against the wooden scaffolds.

    Image prompt

    Jian Chen, a sturdy young man in his twenties wearing rough work clothes and a knit cap over his tied-up queue, swings a heavy iron hammer against cold granite. He stands in the dimly lit, smoke-filled interior of a Sierra Nevada railway tunnel alongside fellow Chinese railroad laborers, who are also striking rock. Flickering lanterns cast long, dramatic shadows. Beyond the tunnel opening, massive snowdrifts press against timber scaffolds, creating a harsh, industrious, and frozen atmosphere.

  3. 1870 Mei-ling and Lin-hua Working

    1870 · family · people: Mei-ling Chen, Lin-hua Chen

    In a narrow Sacramento storefront, Mei-ling and her daughter Lin-hua ran a hand laundry behind a painted sign reading CHEN WASH HOUSE. Steam rose from copper tubs as they pressed white shirts with heavy irons. A row of folded bundles lined a shelf, each tagged with a paper customer's ticket.

    Image prompt

    In the dim, steam-filled interior of a Sacramento laundry, Mei-ling Chen, a sturdy woman in her forties wearing a simple dark linen tunic, presses a white shirt with a heavy iron. Beside her, her daughter Lin-hua Chen, a slender young girl in a faded cotton apron, carefully folds linens near copper tubs. The room is cluttered with hanging laundry and shelves stacked with neat, paper-tagged bundles under the warm glow of a hanging lantern.

  4. 1878 Hong-wei Chen’s Herbal Shop

    1878 · family · people: Hong-wei Chen, elderly customer

    Middle brother Hong-wei opened a small herbalist's shop on Dupont Street in San Francisco's Chinatown. He stood behind a wooden counter fitted with many small drawers, weighing dried ginseng on a brass balance. A painted scroll of medicinal roots hung on the wall, and an old customer waited on a stool.

    Image prompt

    Inside a dim Chinatown apothecary, Hong-wei Chen, a young man in a clean scholar's tunic with a focused expression, carefully measures dried ginseng on a brass balance scale behind a weathered wooden counter of many tiny drawers. A painted botanical scroll hangs on the exposed brick wall. An elderly customer with a hunched back and worn indigo clothing sits patiently on a wooden stool, waiting for his remedy as sunlight filters through a storefront window

  5. 1872 Wei Chen Learns English

    1872 · childhood · people: Wei Chen, Protestant mission teacher, Chinese classmates, Mexican classmates, Anglo classmates

    Seven-year-old Wei sat on a wooden bench at a Protestant mission school in Sacramento, copying English letters from a slate. A white teacher in a high collar walked among a mix of Chinese, Mexican, and Anglo children. Wei's queue hung down his back as he bent over his work.

    Image prompt

    In a sunlit 1872 Sacramento mission classroom, seven-year-old Wei Chen with a long braided queue wears simple cotton tunic clothes while hunched over a wooden bench. Beside him, a severe Protestant mission teacher in a tall lace-trimmed high collar walks down the aisle. Diverse Chinese classmates, Mexican classmates, and Anglo classmates are seated at various desks, all focused on slate boards. The room features peeling plaster walls, antique inkwells, and dust motes dancing in shafts of morning light.

  6. 1882 The Chens Face Exclusion

    1882 · history · people: Hong-wei Chen, Fu-hai Chen, Mei-ling Chen, Jian Chen

    In 1882 the family gathered around the kitchen table as Hong-wei read aloud from a newspaper reporting the Chinese Exclusion Act. Fu-hai, gray-haired now, stared at the stove; Mei-ling folded her hands; Jian, still unmarried, set down a tea cup. A printed broadside with the act's text lay on the table.

    Image prompt

    In a dim 1880s kitchen, Hong-wei Chen, a middle-aged man in a linen shirt, reads from a newspaper. Beside him, gray-haired Fu-hai Chen sits hunched toward the stove, while Mei-ling Chen, an elegant woman in a high-collared blouse, folds her hands. Young Jian Chen, dressed in a simple cotton jacket, rests a ceramic teacup on the wooden table. A printed broadside detailing the Chinese Exclusion Act lies prominently in the center under warm, flickering lamplight.

  7. 1887 Wei Chen at the Vineyard

    1887 · labor · people: Wei Chen, Mexican field workers, Chinese field workers

    Grown now, Wei took work at the Vasquez vineyard in the Napa Valley, once part of a Californio land grant. He carried a wooden basket of purple grapes down a long row of vines toward a waiting mule cart. Mexican and Chinese workers moved along the rows under a hot autumn sun.

    Image prompt

    Under a sweltering autumn sun, Wei Chen, a young man in a worn linen shirt and straw hat, carries a heavy wooden basket overflowing with deep purple grapes through the Napa Valley vines. Nearby, Mexican field workers in weathered trousers and Chinese field workers in traditional loose tunics move rhythmically down the long, dusty rows toward a waiting wooden mule cart. The scene captures the golden light of the vineyard and the labor of the harvest.

  8. 1888 Wei and Dolores Meet

    1888 · courtship · people: Wei Chen, Dolores Vasquez

    Under a great valley oak at the edge of the vineyard, Wei met Dolores Vasquez, the vintner's youngest daughter. She held a clay jug of water for the workers; he had paused, hat in hand, to drink. Behind them, the adobe walls of the old rancho rose against golden hills.

    Image prompt

    Under the expansive shade of a sprawling valley oak, Wei Chen, a slender young man in dusty laborer clothes with a wide-brimmed hat in hand, pauses to drink. Beside him stands Dolores Vasquez, the vintner's youthful daughter, wearing a simple linen dress and holding a rustic clay water jug. Behind them, the sun-drenched adobe walls of an old rancho contrast with rolling, golden California hills under a clear blue sky during the golden hour.

  9. 1889 Wei and Dolores Wed

    1889 · marriage · people: Wei Chen, Dolores Vasquez, mission priest, Lin-hua Chen, Rafael Vasquez

    In 1889 Wei and Dolores married quietly at a small mission chapel in Sonoma, the ceremony conducted in Spanish and Latin. Wei wore a black American suit; Dolores wore a lace mantilla over dark hair. Two witnesses stood beside them: his sister Lin-hua and her elder brother Rafael, the families still uncertain of one another.

    Image prompt

    Inside a dim, sun-dappled mission chapel in Sonoma, Wei Chen, a slender young man in a sharp black suit, stands beside his bride, Dolores Vasquez, who wears a delicate lace mantilla over her dark hair. A bearded mission priest officiates at an altar adorned with flickering candles. Standing slightly apart are Lin-hua Chen, wearing a modest silk dress, and Rafael Vasquez, a tall, stern man in a vest, watching the ceremony with a reserved, watchful expression.

  10. 1895 Wei and Dolores Cooking

    1895 · family · people: Wei Chen, Dolores Vasquez

    In the adobe farmhouse kitchen, Dolores rolled corn tortillas at one end of a long plank table while Wei chopped scallions and ginger at the other. A pot of red mole simmered on one side of the wood stove, a pot of rice porridge on the other. Strings of dried chiles and dried mushrooms hung from the same ceiling beam.

    Image prompt

    In a rustic 1895 adobe kitchen, Wei Chen, a slender middle-aged man in a simple linen shirt, stands at a wooden plank table carefully chopping ginger. Nearby, Dolores Vasquez, a sturdy woman in her thirties wearing a flour-dusted apron over a calico dress, rolls fresh corn tortillas. Sunlight streams through a small window onto the wood stove, where red mole and rice porridge simmer, while dried chiles and mushrooms hang from overhead ceiling beams.

  11. 1906 Hong-wei Flees the Rubble

    1906 · history · people: Hong-wei Chen, Hong-wei's wife, Hong-wei's small children, Fu-hai Chen, Wei Chen

    On the morning of April 18, 1906, the family ran from their San Francisco flat as the city shook and chimneys fell. Hong-wei stood in the street clutching an account book; his wife carried their small children; an elderly Fu-hai leaned on Wei's arm. Smoke rose in black columns over Chinatown behind them.

    Image prompt

    The morning sun is obscured by thick, swirling black smoke in 1906 San Francisco. Hong-wei Chen, a middle-aged man in a rumpled linen shirt, stands in the street clutching a worn leather account book. Nearby, his wife, a young woman in a traditional apron, holds two small children close. Elderly Fu-hai Chen, wearing a frail silk tunic, leans heavily on the arm of young Wei Chen, who looks back toward collapsing brick chimneys in terror.

  12. 1943 The Chen-Vasquez Siblings at the Gate

    1943 · legacy · people: Miguel Chen-Vasquez, Elena Chen-Vasquez, Tomás Chen-Vasquez

    Three grandchildren of Wei and Dolores stood together at the vineyard gate during the Second World War. Miguel wore a U.S. Army uniform bound for the Pacific; Elena wore a nurse's cape, bound for a San Francisco hospital; young Tomás, still in high school, held schoolbooks. Beyond the gate stretched the old rancho's vineyard rows toward the hills.

    Image prompt

    In 1943, three siblings stand at a weathered iron vineyard gate at sunset. Miguel Chen-Vasquez, a tall young man in a crisp olive-drab U.S. Army uniform, gazes toward distant hills. Beside him, Elena Chen-Vasquez, a graceful woman in a dark nurse’s cape and white uniform, looks on somberly. Younger brother Tomás Chen-Vasquez, a lanky teenager wearing a letterman sweater and holding a stack of vintage schoolbooks, stands between them. Golden light illuminates the sprawling vineyard rows.
